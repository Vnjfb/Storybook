Thermal Power Plants

Thermal power plants convert heat energy (usually from coal) into electrical energy. The process involves burning fuel, generating steam, and rotating turbines to produce electricity.

  • Efficiency: Typically 33-40% for coal-fired plants.
  • Environmental Impact: High due to CO2 emissions.
  • Key Formula: Efficiency = (Work Output / Heat Input) × 100

Hydro Power Plants

Hydro power plants use the energy of falling water to rotate turbines and generate electricity. They are renewable and eco-friendly.

  • Efficiency: Typically 85-90%.
  • Key Formula: Power = ρ × g × H × Q × Efficiency
  • ρ: Density of water, g: Gravity, H: Head, Q: Flow rate
