PN Junction Diode

PN junction diodes are the simplest type of diode. They allow current to flow in one direction only when forward biased. Key topics include:

  • Forward and Reverse Bias Characteristics
  • Depletion Region and Barrier Potential
  • Applications in Rectifiers

Zener Diode

Zener diodes are designed to operate in reverse breakdown region. They are used for:

  • Voltage Regulation
  • Overvoltage Protection
  • Shunt Regulation Circuits

LED (Light Emitting Diode)

LEDs emit light when forward biased. Topics include:

  • Working Principle of LEDs
  • Materials Used (e.g., GaAs, GaN)
  • Applications in Lighting and Indicators

Photodiode

Photodiodes are used to detect light and convert it into an electrical signal. Key topics include:

  • Reverse Bias Operation
  • Applications in Optical Communication
  • Characteristics of Photodiodes

Applications of Diodes

Diodes are widely used in:

  • Rectification Circuits (AC to DC conversion)
  • Clipping and Clamping Circuits
  • Voltage Multipliers
  • Signal Demodulation
