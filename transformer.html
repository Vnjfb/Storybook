Working Principles of Transformer

Transformers work on the principle of electromagnetic induction. They consist of two coils (primary and secondary) wound around a core. When an alternating current flows through the primary coil, it creates a magnetic field, which induces a voltage in the secondary coil.

Components of Transformer

  • Core: Made of laminated sheets to reduce eddy currents.
  • Primary Coil: Receives input voltage.
  • Secondary Coil: Delivers output voltage.
  • Insulation: Prevents short circuits between coils and ensures safe operation.

Efficiency of Transformer

The efficiency of a transformer is given by the ratio of the output power to the input power. It is typically very high (95% or more), but losses still occur due to factors such as resistance, eddy currents, and hysteresis.

Applications of Transformers

Transformers are used in a wide range of applications such as:

  • Power transmission and distribution networks.
  • Step-up and step-down voltage conversion in electrical systems.
  • Voltage regulation in electronic devices.

Advantages and Disadvantages of Transformers

  • Advantages: Efficient power transmission, voltage regulation, and cost-effectiveness.
  • Disadvantages: Bulky, requires cooling, and efficiency losses at high load conditions.
