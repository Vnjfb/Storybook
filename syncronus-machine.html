Working Principles of Synchronous Machines

Synchronous machines operate at a constant speed. The rotor rotates in synchronization with the stator's rotating magnetic field, which is created by the AC supply.

Components of Synchronous Machine

  • Stator: The stationary part of the machine, which generates the rotating magnetic field.
  • Rotor: The rotating part that is powered by the stator's magnetic field.
  • Field Windings: Produce the magnetic field in the rotor and are connected to the power supply.

Efficiency of Synchronous Machine

The efficiency of synchronous machines is calculated by the ratio of electrical output power to the input power. It is typically high, but losses still occur due to factors like friction and windage.

Applications of Synchronous Machines

  • Synchronous generators are used in power plants for electricity generation.
  • Synchronous motors are used in industries for precise speed control.

Advantages and Disadvantages of Synchronous Machines

  • Advantages: High efficiency, constant speed operation, and high power factor.
  • Disadvantages: Requires complex control systems, high maintenance, and is not suitable for variable loads.
