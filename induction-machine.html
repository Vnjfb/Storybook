Working Principles of Induction Machines

Induction motors work on the principle of electromagnetic induction. When current flows through the stator, it creates a rotating magnetic field that induces a current in the rotor. This current produces a magnetic field, which interacts with the stator field, creating motion.

Components of Induction Machine

  • Stator: The stationary part that generates a rotating magnetic field.
  • Rotor: The rotating part that interacts with the stator field to produce torque.
  • Squirrel Cage: The most common rotor type used in induction motors, consisting of conductive bars.

Efficiency of Induction Machine

Applications of Induction Machines

Induction motors are widely used for various applications such as:

  • Electric fans, pumps, and compressors.
  • Large industrial drives, such as conveyors and elevators.
  • Welding machines, cranes, and agricultural equipment.

Advantages and Disadvantages of Induction Machines

  • Advantages: Robust construction, low maintenance, and cost-effectiveness.
  • Disadvantages: Low power factor, efficiency losses under variable load, and non-constant speed.
